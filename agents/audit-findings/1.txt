Incompatible CSI index reinterpretation without migration in VTSFeeLib upgrade causes unfair fee allocation and self-bonus recapture

The PR changes CSI accounting from an additive spent index to a multiplicative remaining-share factor but reuses existing storage without migration. On state-preserving upgrades, legacy indices are misinterpreted, understating or overstating positions’ selfRemaining shares, enabling positions to claim bonuses from pots that still include their own slashed contributions and distorting future allocations.

Pre-PR, feesSharedSpendIndexX128 and feesSharedIndexLastX128 stored an additive “spent-per-share” accumulator; positions burned remaining self-shares by subtracting sharesRemaining * (indexNow - indexLast) / Q128. The PR redefines these fields to a multiplicative “remaining-share factor” and introduces feesSharedEpoch, but does not migrate legacy values. In _syncFeesSharedRemainingForToken, epochLast is coerced to 1 (to bypass epoch mismatch), and indexNow/indexLast are applied as multiplicative factors even though they still hold old additive values. This mis-scales selfRemaining: for indexLast==0, a legacy 0.2*Q128 (20% spent) is treated as 20% remaining; when both indices are nonzero, shares can be incorrectly increased via indexNow/indexLast > 1. _advanceFeesSharedFactor also uses the old additive index as factorBase, anchoring the new epoch’s factor to the wrong baseline and propagating distortion. Because bonus eligibility excludes a position’s own contribution via potAvail = pot - selfRemaining, understated selfRemaining lets a position reclaim from a pot that still embeds its own slashed contributions. VTSFeeLinkedLib.beforeFeeShareMint is called before increasing feesShared and protocolFeeAccrued, ensuring the first post-upgrade sync applies the new math to legacy values. This yields unfair fee payouts (draining slashedPot) and persistent misallocation after upgrade.
