Seizing-flag RFS gate bypass in VTSPositionLib during MM decreases with pause sign-gating allows premature withdrawal and queue creation

Negative-liquidity MM operations are allowed during pause and VTSPositionLib._touchExistingDecrease skips the RFS-open revert when isSeizing=true, letting an MM router decrease while RFS is open, shrink commitmentMax, close RFS, and then withdraw and/or pre-queue settlement claims, including during pause.

CoreHook._afterRemoveLiquidity now always calls into VTS processing, and pause is enforced in VTSPositionLib.touchPosition only as a sign check (blocks liquidityDelta >= 0, allows < 0). In VTSPositionLib._touchExistingDecrease, the RFS gate was relaxed: it reverts on RFS-open only if NOT isSeizing. With isSeizing=true in hookData, a decrease proceeds even when RFS is open. The decrease path immediately reduces commitmentMax via _trackCommitment. Because getRFS computes required settled using commitmentMax (base = baseVTSRate × c, clamped by c and plus commitment deficits), shrinking c reduces requirements and can close RFS. During the same decrease, _processMMOperations stages LCC cancellations and queues any shortfall via LiquidityHub.planCancelWithQueue keyed to the provided locker, and accounts DynamicCurrencyDelta for settleable portions (clamped by MarketVault.dryModifyLiquidities). The attacker can then call onMMSettle with isSeizing=false: growth is settled, RFS is now closed (after the attacker’s own c shrink), and withdrawals are allowed up to vault availability, while queued claims remain for later settlement. validateMMOperation enforces advancer authority only for non-seizing operations; seizing decreases do not require advancer locker nor seizability proof at decrease time. This enables premature extraction of vault liquidity and lock-in of settlement queue claims during pause or while RFS was initially open.
