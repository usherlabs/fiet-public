4.2.6. Fee On Transfer Reserve Inflation
Unit Name: LiquidityHub
Location: contracts/evm/src/LiquidityHub.sol — _wrap internal function
Description: The _wrap function credits s.directSupply[lcc] += amount and
s.reserveOfUnderlying[underlying].direct += amount based on the requested transfer amount
parameter, not the actual amount of tokens received by the contract. The transfer is
performed via Currency.wrap(underlying).transferFrom(from, address(this), amount) which uses
Solady's safeTransferFrom2 , but no balance-before/after check is performed to verify the
actual received amount.
If a fee-on-transfer, deflationary, or rebasing ERC20 token is used as the underlying asset
for an LCC, the actual tokens received will be less than amount . However, the reserve
accounting and LCC minting both credit the full amount . This inflates directSupply and
reserveOfUnderlying.direct beyond the actual token balance held by the contract.
The protocol's threat model states "the protocol detects and reverts on such tokens" but no
such detection mechanism exists in the _wrap function or the CurrencyTransfer.transferFrom
library call. While confirmTakeBalanceInvariant provides a post-hoc check in the confirmTake
flow, _wrap has no equivalent balance invariant assertion. The inflated accounting allows
users to receive more LCC tokens than the underlying they deposited, potentially enabling
them to unwrap more underlying than their actual contribution.
This violates the Domain A wrapping conservation invariant: "directSupply[lcc] and
reserveOfUnderlying[underlying] must be incremented by exactly the amount of underlying
actually received."
User Verdict: True Positive
