4.2.9. Subscriber Dos On Modify Liquidity
Unit Name: PositionManager
Location: contracts/evm/lib/v4-periphery/src/PositionManager.sol _modifyLiquidity function;
contracts/evm/lib/v4-periphery/src/base/Notifier.sol _notifyModifyLiquidity and _call
functions
Description: The _modifyLiquidity function calls _notifyModifyLiquidity when a position has a
subscriber ( info.hasSubscriber() is true). The _notifyModifyLiquidity function in Notifier.sol
invokes the subscriber's notifyModifyLiquidity callback via _call() , which uses a low-level
call(gas(), target, 0, ...) that forwards ALL remaining gas to the subscriber with no gas
limit. If the subscriber's callback reverts, _notifyModifyLiquidity calls bubbleUpAndRevertWith with
ModifyLiquidityNotificationReverted , reverting the entire liquidity modification transaction.
This creates a DoS vector aﬀecting _increase (INCREASE_LIQUIDITY), _increaseFromDeltas
(INCREASE_LIQUIDITY_FROM_DELTAS), and _decrease (DECREASE_LIQUIDITY) — all of which
route through _modifyLiquidity . These are the most commonly-used liquidity operations,
making this a higher-impact DoS surface than the already-identified burn DoS.
In the Fiet protocol, the subscriber is DirectLPDeltaResolver , whose notifyModifyLiquidity calls
_afterModifyLiquidity(tokenId) , which in turn calls factory.afterModifyLiquidity(poolKey) . Inside
MarketFactory.afterModifyLiquidity , there is a Bounds.isEndpoint() check that reverts with
Errors.InvalidSender() if the DirectLPDeltaResolver is not a registered endpoint. If the factory's
bounds are revoked, the factory is replaced, or CoreHook.settleHookDeltasToPot(key) reverts, ALL
subscribed positions in that pool become unable to perform any liquidity modifications.
A workaround exists: position owners can call unsubscribe(tokenId) first (which uses gas-
limited try/catch and will succeed even if the subscriber is broken), then perform the
liquidity modification. This can be done atomically via multicall([unsubscribe(tokenId),
modifyLiquidities(data, deadline)]) . However, this requires knowledge of the workaround,
causes loss of the subscriber relationship (and thus loss of DirectLPDeltaResolver accounting
integration), and adds operational complexity.
This is distinct from the previously identified subscriber_dos_on_burn finding, which covers the
_removeSubscriberAndNotifyBurn path used during _burn . This finding covers the
_notifyModifyLiquidity path used during all other liquidity operations.
User Verdict: True Positive
