Non-atomic reserve pre-check with permissionless settlement in MMPositionManager._collectAvailableLiquidity causes revert/gas grief and temporary liveness loss

MMPositionManager._collectAvailableLiquidity optimistically reads LiquidityHub’s market-derived reserve, releases LCC from a shared custodian, then calls processSettlementFor. Because settlement is permissionless and the shared market-derived reserve is raceable, others can consume reserves between the read and the call. If reserves drop to zero, the call reverts (gas grief); if reduced but nonzero, settlement succeeds partially. No funds are lost due to atomic reverts.

The collection path in MMPositionManager._collectAvailableLiquidity reads queued, market-derived reserve (reserveOfUnderlyingTuple’s second value), and custodied amounts to compute a toSettle, releases that much LCC from MMQueueCustodian to the locker, then immediately calls LiquidityHub.processSettlementFor(lcc, locker, released). LiquidityHubLib.processSettlementLogic clamps settlement to real-time values and reverts only when the computed toSettle is zero. Because processSettlementFor is permissionless and the market-derived reserve is shared across participants, another actor can front-run and settle their own queues first, reducing the available reserve between the pre-check and the settlement call. If available becomes zero, the call reverts, rolling back the custodian release (gas grief, temporary liveness failure). If available remains > 0, settlement partially succeeds and the remainder stays with the locker for later settlement. This behavior is introduced/heightened by the PR’s new collection path through a shared MMQueueCustodian and reliance on the split-reserve model’s marketDerived component.
