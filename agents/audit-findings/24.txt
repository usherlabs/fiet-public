Missing PoolManager unlock in LCC ingress settlement on transfers to DEX-bound PoolManager causes transfer reverts

LCC transfers to the Uniswap v4 PoolManager (DEX-bound) trigger an ingress settlement path that calls PoolManager.settle/mint while locked, reverting with ManagerLocked and denying these transfers.

When LCC tokens are transferred to a DEX-bound, bucket-exempt recipient (the Uniswap v4 PoolManager), LCC._beforeTransfer now invokes IMarketFactory.prepareMarketLiquidity(lcc, wrappedAmount). MarketFactory.prepareMarketLiquidity forwards to MarketLiquidityRouterLib.prepareMarketLiquidityIngress, which calls the canonical vault handler’s handleIngress(lcc, wrappedAmount). The handler (ProxyHook/MarketVault) attempts to settle underlying to the PoolManager using CurrencySettler.settle and then mint ERC6909 claim tokens using CurrencySettler.take. These ultimately call PoolManager.settle and PoolManager.mint, which require the PoolManager to be unlocked. Because this ingress flow is executed during a plain ERC20 transfer (outside any poolManager.unlock callback), PoolManager is typically locked, causing a ManagerLocked revert and denying the transfer. This behavior was introduced by the new ingress routing and by setting PoolManager as a DEX sink. Normal ProxyHook-led protocol flows remain unaffected because exempt senders do not trigger prepareMarketLiquidity.
