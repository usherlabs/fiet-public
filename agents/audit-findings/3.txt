Paused remove-liquidity bypasses RFS gating in CoreHook/VTSPositionLib causing obligation shrink and potential improper withdrawals

When the market or pool is paused, remove-liquidity is routed through a new reconcile path that skips the normal RFS-closure check, allowing active under-settled positions to decrease liquidity without seizure, shrinking commitment-based obligations and enabling value extraction (direct LPs can unwrap to underlying).

During pause, CoreHook._afterRemoveLiquidity now calls VTSOrchestrator.reconcileAfterPausedRemove, which delegates to VTSPositionLib.reconcileAfterPausedRemove. This path enforces only negative liquidity delta, canonical core-hook caller, and pause-active checks, then: (1) reduces per-position commitment maxima via _trackCommitment; (2) clamps settled only if it exceeds the reduced maxima; and (3) updates the liquidity mirror. Critically, it does not call calcRFS(..., true) and therefore does not revert when a position’s RFS is open. In contrast, the normal (unpaused) decrease path runs VTSPositionLib._touchExistingDecrease, which calls calcRFS with requireClosedRfS=true and reverts if RFS is open for non-seizure withdrawals. By bypassing this gate during pause, an attacker can reduce liquidity to shrink commitment-based requirements (baseVTS and capped deficits) and, for direct LPs, receive LCC and unwrap to underlying via LiquidityHub (which is not paused), enabling improper withdrawal while RFS is open. For MMs, immediate underlying extraction may be delayed because planned-cancel is not staged in the paused path, but the accounting benefit (shrunk obligations and seizure exposure) is immediate.
