Deposit-time residual CISE flush in VTSPositionLib (0→>0 totalSettled) enables first-depositor to capture full bonus pot

A PR-added residual CISE flush on 0→>0 totalSettled transitions lets the first depositor immediately socialise all deferred coverage into the CISE index and pool exposure window, then crystallise and allocate 100% of the fee pot share by sequencing public calls. Subsequent LPs lose expected bonus yield.

The PR modifies VTSPositionLib._updatePoolAccounting to flush coverageResidualCISE when pool.totalSettled for a token transitions from 0 to >0 during a deposit. This pushes the residual CISE socialisation to deposit-time (attacker-controlled) rather than coverage-increment-time. A first depositor can (1) add minimal direct-LP liquidity to trigger the flush, (2) call settlePositionGrowths to realise the entire exposure window as their own (permissionless while unpaused), and (3) perform a no-op modify to trigger fee processing and allocate 100% of protocolFeeAccrued for the opposite token (self-exclusion does not reduce potAvail for a fresh position). If slashedPot is funded, payout is immediate; otherwise the allocation still reduces protocolFeeAccrued and records a negative pending claim, depriving future LPs of bonuses. This creates a miner-orderable economic window that unfairly concentrates historic residual-based bonuses into the first post-zero depositor.
