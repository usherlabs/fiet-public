Paused remove-liquidity skips fee materialization in CoreHook causes slash/fee adjustment evasion and slashed-pot underfunding

When the market is paused, CoreHook’s remove-liquidity flow returns zero hook delta and skips VTS fee materialization, while still settling growths that create positive pending slashes. Because Uniswap v4 nets the hook delta into callerDelta, removing during pause lets the remover avoid the immediate fee adjustment that would otherwise reduce their proceeds, and the corresponding slashed-pot funding is never materialized if the position is abandoned.

Under pause, CoreHook._beforeRemoveLiquidity still calls VTSOrchestrator.settlePositionGrowths, which triggers VTSPositionLib.settlePositionGrowths and DICE coverage burns via VTSFeeLib._applyBurnBase. This increases protocolFeeAccrued, per-position feesShared, and per-position pendingFeeAdj (a positive slash). However, CoreHook._afterRemoveLiquidity in pause mode skips VTSOrchestrator.processPosition (which normally calls VTSFeeLib._finaliseFeeAdjustment to materialize pending slashes) and returns a zero hook delta. Uniswap v4 returns callerDelta already net of the hook delta; with zero hook delta, the remover’s callerDelta is not reduced in that call. The positive pending slash remains on the position and is only materialized by a later position touch (processPosition); if the position is abandoned, those slashes remain unmaterialized indefinitely. This deprives the slashed/bonus pot and shifts fee value to the remover compared to the unpaused baseline. Pre-PR, all removes went through processPosition and could not skip fee materialization.
