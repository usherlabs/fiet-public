4.2.4. Balance Sync Overcrediting
Unit Name: MMPositionManager
Location: contracts/evm/src/MMPositionManager.sol, _unwrapLccFromDeltas and
_unwrapLccFromUser functions, specifically the _syncBalanceAsCredit call when to == address(this)
and underlying is an ERC20
Description: In _unwrapLccFromDeltas and _unwrapLccFromUser , when to == address(this) (i.e., the
unwrapped underlying tokens are sent to the MMPositionManager contract itself), the credit
accounting diﬀers between native ETH and ERC20 underlyings. For native ETH,
_creditExact(CurrencyLibrary.ADDRESS_ZERO, unwrapped) credits the exact unwrapped amount. For
ERC20 underlyings, _syncBalanceAsCredit(Currency.wrap(underlying)) is called, which invokes
DynamicCurrencyDelta.syncBalanceAsCredit . This function computes the credit as balance -
currentDelta (the diﬀerence between the contract's full ERC20 balance and the current
delta).
The syncBalanceAsCredit approach can attribute more tokens than the operation actually
produced. If the MMPositionManager holds residual ERC20 balance from a previous
operation in the same batch (or from unsolicited token donations), the balance-based sync
would credit those residual tokens to the current locker as well. While the assertNonZeroDeltas
check in _afterBatch provides a safety net requiring all deltas to net out, and in normal
operation the sync incremental accounting should work correctly, the asymmetric handling
between native ETH (exact crediting) and ERC20 (balance-based crediting) creates an
inconsistency that could be exploited in edge cases involving multi-step batches where
intermediate ERC20 balances are not fully consumed.
User Verdict: True Positive
